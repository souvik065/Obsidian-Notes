# Why it rings true

- **Asymmetry of investment:** You describe a relationship where the emotional ledger is one-sided — you give sacrifice, time, effort; they give little back. That pattern erodes attraction and dignity over time.
    
- **Actions > words:** People can promise reciprocity, but consistent action is the real test. Repeated small refusals to invest are louder than one grand declaration.
    
- **Attachment vs. mutuality:** Loving deeply is not the problem — loving someone who cannot or will not meet you halfway is. That mismatch creates grief, not proof of failure.
    
- **Slow erosion:** The pain rarely arrives in a single moment. It’s the accumulation of repeated tiny imbalances — exactly what your passage describes.
